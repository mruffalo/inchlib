Glucocorticoid receptor (GR) belongs to a larger family of nuclear receptors. GR cognate ligands are cortisol and other glucocorticoids. GR is a ubiquitously expressed intracellular protein which functions as a ligand-dependent transcription factor and regulates genes controlling the development, metabolism, and immune response.

The displayed data set consists of 7 physico-chemical and structural properties of 372 GR agonists obtained from the ChEMBL database. The ligand properties are: logarithm of the octanol-water partition coefficient (logP), molar refractivity (SMR), topological polar surface area (TPSA), molecular weight, and the numbers of rotatable bonds, hydrogen-bond donors, hydrogen-bond acceptors and aromatic rings. Each ligand is characterized by its EC50 (the concentration which induces 50% of the maximum biological response) displayed in the red metadata column.

The data were normalized to (0,1) scale and clustered using Euclidean distance and Ward's linkage. In the heatmap, raw data (i.e., before normalization) are displayed. The structure visualization was created using cheminformatics programming package RDKit.

Data set information
  • instances: 372 GR ligands
  • features: 7 physico-chemical and structural properties
  • metadata: EC50 values of ligands
Source: ChEMBL - Glucocorticoid receptor
